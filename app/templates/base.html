The Prevelace of the Himalayas

The Himalayas are known for their jewel-like crescents that tower the Asian continent. However, its existence is of the utmost importance. An example of this is the country of India. India's major rivers begin their journey in the Himalayas. Its climate influence brings monsoons to India. It creates some of the most fertile of lands, filled with lush forests. The Himalayas bring minerals to the riverbanks of India. With these material benefits, there are other types of benefits. The Himalayas are one of the most auspicious sanctums of religious purposes. Lined with shrines to worship and meditate at, Himalayas provide a calm environment. Known as the "The Land of Snow" by the regional languages, it is one of, if not the, most famous mountain range. The Peak of the Planet

Mount Everest, known to local Nepaleans as Sagarmatha, meaning 'Goddess of the Sky'. Mount Everest stands at 29,029 feet (8,848 meters) above sea level, making it the highest mountain on Earth. Its sheer scale and beauty is one that many marvel as the power of nature. Climbing Mount Everest is a feat not for the fain of heart. More than 300 people have passed away climbing the peak. Its frost landscape and the jaged rocks makes it a treacherous climb. But, if one were to climb it, they would reach the literal pinnacle of the planet, gazing upon the opaque snow that blankets the Himalayas.
